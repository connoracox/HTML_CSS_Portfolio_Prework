About Me

My name is Connor Cox, I grew up in Houston, TX. I spent the last 5 years living in Seattle, WA and recently moved to the Northeast to Stamford, CT. I graduated college in 2016 with a Bachelor's Degree in Advertising & Public Relations from the University of Tampa. Most of my working background is in sales in the tech industry and natural foods industry.

I have completed two long-distance backpacking trips (or thru-hikes) in the United States; the Continental Divide Trail in 2022, and the Pacific Crest Trail in 2017.

I enjoy the outdoors, plants, and cooking. My favorite outdoor activities are backpacking, running, rock climbing, and skiing. I love baking bread and trying new cooking recipes.

Why coding?

I am quite new to coding and Software Engineering, I took one course in Web Design in the second semester of my senior year of undergrad. Although I enjoyed the course, I did not have plans to continue with my education at that time.

After trying my best in a career in Sales, I ultimately decided that it was not the right long-term fit for me. I decided that I would go back to school in one form or another. Like everyone, I have a few friends who are Software Engineers or work in Tech in general. After spending the summer backpacking the Continental Divide Trail with several fellow hikers who happened to be coders, I got to know a lot more about Software Engineering and it certainly piqued my interest.

I want to learn full-stack developing so that I can learn about all different types of coding and grow a diverse and complete skillset to set myself up for a successful career in computer programming.
